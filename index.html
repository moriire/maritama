Maritama Logistics, explained the meaning of Contract of Carriage.

A bill of lading (B/L) is a legal document issued by a carrier, such as a shipping line, freight forwarder, or logistics company, to acknowledge the receipt of goods for shipment. It serves as evidence of the contract of carriage between the shipper (consignor) and the carrier, and it provides details about the goods being transported. The bill of lading serves several important functions: 1. Receipt of Goods: It serves as a receipt for the goods, confirming that they have been received by the carrier in good condition and in the specified quantity. 2. Contract of Carriage: It acts as a contract between the shipper and the carrier, outlining the terms and conditions of the transportation, such as the agreed-upon freight rate, the destination, and any special instructions or requirements. 3. Title of Goods: In some cases, the bill of lading can also function as a document of title, representing ownership of the goods. It can be used for negotiating, transferring, or financing the goods during their journey. 4. Document of Delivery: Upon arrival at the destination, the bill of lading is used to transfer the goods to the recipient (consignee) and serves as proof of delivery.
